[0:00 - INTRODUCTION]

At its heart, SprintPilot AI is an AI-powered Business Planning and Project Execution Assistant. It’s designed to help founders, product owners, and agile software teams turn a raw business idea into a fully scaffolded, launch-ready project blueprint in a single click."

[0:30 - ABOUT ME]
"To give you a quick bit of background, my name is [Your Name], and as a developer, I've spent years watching startup founders and product teams struggle with the initial phase of launching projects. Writing business strategies, planning software specs, compiling user stories, mapping risks—it's a massive, exhausting chore. That's why I built SprintPilot AI: to fully automate that path."

[0:55 - PROBLEM STATEMENT]
"We’ve all seen it: a founder gets a brilliant business idea. But moving from that initial idea to a structured backlog of engineering requirements takes weeks. You have to draft a business outline, translate it into technical specifications, write user story cards, schedule milestone roadmaps, map operational risks, and compile investor summaries. The process is slow, fragmented, and there's always a huge translation gap between business vision and engineering execution."

[1:30 - WHY AI AGENTS?]
"Normally, if you use a standard chatbot, you have to prompt it step-by-step. You ask for a business plan. Then you copy that output and ask it to write requirements. Then you ask for user stories. It takes forever, and the chatbot frequently forgets context. 

SprintPilot AI uses an autonomous agentic pipeline. It acts as an orchestrator. When you give it an idea, it scans for necessary parameters like the company name, sector, target market, and budget. If anything is missing, it asks you up front. Once it has them, it triggers the entire execution pipeline sequentially—passing output data to the next tool automatically, with zero human steering needed."

[2:10 - SPRINTPILOT ARCHITECTURE]
"Let’s take a look at the architecture. 

SprintPilot AI is built natively on the Google Agent Development Kit (ADK) 2.0. When an input enters the FastAPI backend, it is processed by the RootAgent orchestrator. 

If all parameters are complete, the RootAgent triggers our sequential pipeline wrapper. This wrapper coordinates seven specialized tools: formulating the value proposition, generating technical requirements (PRDs), drafting Scrum user stories, scheduling a weekly roadmap, conducting risk registry audits, compiling software documentation, and creating a VC-ready executive summary. 

Additionally, the RootAgent is connected to Model Context Protocol (MCP) clients, enabling it to write files to the local filesystem, post GitHub issues, upload documents to Google Drive, and schedule milestone events on Google Calendar."

I’ll type in: 'I want to build a software consultancy startup named DevSprint in the SaaS industry.'

I'll reply: 'Our target audience is enterprise software buyers, and our initial budget is fifty thousand dollars.'

As soon as I hit submit, look at the logs. SprintPilot AI is running the sequential pipeline. We see live progress indicators:

- The Requirements Generator maps the PRD.
- The User Story Generator drafts Scrum cards.
- The Roadmap Generator schedulers weekly epics.
- The Risk Analysis registry assesses legal and operational hazards.
- The Documentation Generator compiles code frameworks.
- The Executive Summary synthesizes investor metrics.

And here is the final response. It is the complete, compiled Operations Report printed verbatim directly in our chat window—including the milestone tables, competitor matrices, and mitigation plans. No abbreviations, no summaries, no manual copy-pasting."
